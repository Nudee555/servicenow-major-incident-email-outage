# ServiceNow Major Incident Management Lab – Enterprise Email Outage

## Project Overview

This project simulates the handling of a company-wide email outage using ServiceNow Major Incident Management processes.

The scenario involved multiple departments reporting email failures, authentication issues, and communication disruptions affecting business operations. The objective was to document the complete incident lifecycle from initial reporting through final resolution while following industry-standard incident management practices.

---

## Business Scenario

A widespread email outage impacted critical business functions across multiple departments including:

- Sales
- Finance
- Human Resources
- Operations
- Executive Leadership

Users reported:

- Inability to send email
- Inability to receive email
- Repeated credential prompts
- Authentication failures
- Service interruptions

Monitoring systems simultaneously generated alerts indicating issues within the enterprise messaging infrastructure.

---

## Major Incident Workflow

### Phase 1 – Incident Detection

- Multiple users contacted the Help Desk
- Monitoring systems generated alerts
- Initial ticket created in ServiceNow
- Business impact assessed

### Phase 2 – Incident Assessment

- Enterprise-wide scope confirmed
- Major Incident criteria met
- Incident escalated
- Messaging, Infrastructure, and Network teams engaged

### Phase 3 – Investigation

Teams performed:

- Log review
- Authentication analysis
- Infrastructure validation
- Root cause investigation

Communication updates were provided throughout the investigation.

### Phase 4 – Resolution

The root cause was identified as an authentication service failure affecting enterprise email systems.

Corrective actions included:

- Service restoration
- Authentication recovery
- Infrastructure validation
- Functional testing

### Phase 5 – Verification

Verification activities included:

- Email send testing
- Email receive testing
- User confirmation
- Monitoring validation

Normal service operation was successfully restored.
